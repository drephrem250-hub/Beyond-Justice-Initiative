The Systemic Blind Spot

The Justice Gap

In Rwanda, statutory rape trials are pursued with high legal resolve, and court judgments routinely order perpetrators to pay child support.

However, **once the trial ends, post-judgment follow-up is non-existent**. Without enforcement, court orders are ignored, leaving the young mother to bear the economic, physical, and psychological burden alone. This breaks her education link, forces school dropouts, and stunts the child's development.

⚠️ **Our Innovation**: We close this gap by building a data-driven accountability pipeline that links legal outcomes to community health workers, local government, and targeted school reintegration.

Who We Are
Our Story
📖 Background & Institutional Mandate
Beyond Justice Initiative (Rinda Mwana) was established as an independent youth-led professional NGO based in Rwanda to address the critical post-judgment enforcement gap in statutory rape and teenage pregnancy cases. Operating in close proximity to academic institutions (University of Rwanda) and local government authorities, we merge field-based data aggregation with localized survivor support.
Our role begins after court verdicts are declared. We focus entirely on accountability—ensuring ordered support is delivered, teen mothers are safely reintegrated into academic and socio-economic systems, and systemic evidence is generated to drive long-term legislative reforms.
🎯 Our Vision
A Rwanda where post-judgment justice is fully realized—ensuring that every teenager affected by sexual violence has access to court-enforced child support, academic reintegration, nutritional care, and full social dignity.
💡 Our Core Principles
  • ✓ Data Integrity: Objective, evidence-led advocacy.
  • ✓ Survivor-First: Active protection and dignity at all stages.
  • ✓ Relentless Accountability: Enforcing systems that promise support.
  • ✓ Youth Leadership: Mobilizing young professionals for systemic changes.
👥 Institutional Infrastructure
Beyond Justice Initiative operates as a professional youth-led NGO, coordinating closely with local government ministries, justice bodies (such as RIB), and academic entities (University of Rwanda, Huye Campus) to run pilot project databases and localized support pipelines across Huye District.

Advocacy
Policy Recommendations
⚖️
1. Mandatory Child Support Enforcement
Clear, monitored procedures ensuring court-ordered support is paid consistently, with penalties for non-compliance.
🔄
2. Post-Court Case Monitoring
Assign district-level responsibility to track real-life outcomes after court rulings, not just during trial.
🎓
3. Education Reintegration
Clear formal policies enabling teen mothers to return to school without administrative barriers or stigma.
🏘️
4. Community Support Programs
Fund community-led structures assisting vulnerable mothers, especially where fathers are absent or imprisoned.
🤝
5. Cross-Sector Coordination
Mandate coordination between justice, social protection, health and education for each documented case.
📢
6. Anti-Stigma Policies
Protect teen mothers from discrimination in schools, health facilities, and communities through enforceable policies.
