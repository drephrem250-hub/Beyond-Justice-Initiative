The Core Problem
"Justice ends in the courtroom. The burden continues at home."
When a verdict is handed down, the system considers its job done. But for a teen mother in Huye — still without support, still out of school, still alone — justice never truly arrived. This is the gap we're addressing: the invisible wall between legal verdicts and real-life support that changes lives.

Who We Are
Our Story
📖 Background
Beyond Justice Initiative was born from a simple observation: the legal system is not enough. Court verdicts matter, but they don't feed children, get mothers back to school, or break cycles of poverty. We started as a youth-led effort in Huye District, Rwanda, to track what happens after the gavel falls—and to fill the gaps the system leaves behind.
🎯 Our Vision
A Rwanda where teen mothers and children affected by injustice receive not just legal protection, but real, measurable support—access to education, health services, economic opportunity, and dignity.
💡 Our Values
  • ✓ Evidence-driven: Data, not assumptions
  • ✓ Survivor-centered: Listen to those affected
  • ✓ Accountability: Systems must deliver on promises
  • ✓ Youth-led: Young people designing solutions
👥 The Team
Beyond Justice Initiative is part of GLF (Global Leadership Forum) — Huye, a youth-led collective dedicated to systemic change in Rwanda. Our team includes field volunteers, coordinators, and community partners working across Huye District to track cases, provide support, and advocate for policy change grounded in real data.

Advocacy
Policy Recommendations
⚖️
1. Mandatory Child Support Enforcement
Clear, monitored procedures ensuring court-ordered support is paid consistently, with penalties for non-compliance.
🔄
2. Post-Court Case Monitoring
Assign district-level responsibility to track real-life outcomes after court rulings, not just during trial.
🎓
3. Education Reintegration
Clear formal policies enabling teen mothers to return to school without administrative barriers or stigma.
🏘️
4. Community Support Programs
Fund community-led structures assisting vulnerable mothers, especially where fathers are absent or imprisoned.
🤝
5. Cross-Sector Coordination
Mandate coordination between justice, social protection, health and education for each documented case.
📢
6. Anti-Stigma Policies
Protect teen mothers from discrimination in schools, health facilities, and communities through enforceable policies.
